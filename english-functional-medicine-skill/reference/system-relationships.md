# System Relationships and Evidence Needs

> **Purpose.** Frame relationships among seven physiological systems
> (`assimilation`, `defense_and_repair`, `structural_integrity`, `energy`,
> `communication`, `transport`, `biotransformation_and_elimination`), the central
> MES (`Mental · Emotional · Spiritual`), and five lifestyle pillars (`sleep`,
> `movement`, `nutrition`, `stress`, `relationships`) as **questions and evidence
> needs**, not as asserted root causes. This document supports the functional
> medicine matrix worksheet; it does not describe a radial graph or system-to-system
> causal map.

---

## 1  How to use this document

When a synthetic teaching scenario raises observations in more than one system,
ask the relationship questions below. Each question ends with an **evidence need**
that must be resolved before any connection can be treated as more than a
provisional hypothesis.

---

## 2  Digestive system ↔ other systems

### 2.1  Digestion ↔ immune system

| Question | Evidence need |
|---|---|
| Is intestinal permeability hypothesized to increase immune exposure to food or microbial antigens? | Independent measurement of permeability; reproducibility of the assay; comparison with healthy reference ranges. |
| Is low stomach acid or pancreatic insufficiency reducing protein digestion? | Clinical evaluation by a qualified provider; not inferable from symptoms alone. |
| Is the gut microbiota imbalanced in a way that could affect immune tone? | Stool testing methodology, population norms, and uncertainty about causality. |

### 2.2  Digestion ↔ neurobehavioral system

| Question | Evidence need |
|---|---|
| Are gut symptoms reported alongside mood, cognitive, or sleep changes? | Temporal relationship; confounders such as stress, sleep, medications. |
| Is the enteric nervous system or vagus signaling a plausible mediator? | Mechanistic studies only; human clinical translation must be verified. |

### 2.3  Digestion ↔ detoxification

| Question | Evidence need |
|---|---|
| Does increased toxin or endotoxin load from the gut challenge liver biotransformation? | Markers of liver function and toxin exposure; Phase I/II balance is a research concept, not a routine clinical test. |

## 3  Immune system ↔ other systems

### 3.1  Immune ↔ metabolic

| Question | Evidence need |
|---|---|
| Is low-grade inflammation associated with insulin resistance or adipose tissue dysfunction? | CRP, cytokines, fasting insulin/glucose; interpretation by a clinician. |
| Does immune activation alter lipid metabolism or vascular inflammation? | Lipid particle analysis only if clinically indicated; population data required. |

### 3.2  Immune ↔ endocrine

| Question | Evidence need |
|---|---|
| Does stress/cortisol shift immune balance (e.g., Th1/Th2 in textbook framing)? | Salivary or serum cortisol patterns; immune phenotyping; these are research-level questions. |
| Is vitamin D status relevant to immune regulation? | 25(OH)D measurement and clinical reference range; supplementation requires clinician oversight. |

### 3.3  Immune ↔ structural

| Question | Evidence need |
|---|---|
| Is autoimmune activity contributing to tissue damage? | Autoantibodies, imaging, specialist evaluation. |

---

## 4  Metabolic system ↔ other systems

### 4.1  Metabolic ↔ endocrine

| Question | Evidence need |
|---|---|
| Is insulin resistance influencing sex-hormone binding globulin or ovarian androgen production? | Fasting insulin, glucose, HbA1c, SHBG, androgen panel; clinical interpretation. |
| Is thyroid conversion (T4→T3) affected by stress, inflammation, or nutrient deficiency? | TSH, free T4, free T3, reverse T3, selenium/iron/zinc status; endocrine evaluation. |

### 4.2  Metabolic ↔ cardiovascular

| Question | Evidence need |
|---|---|
| Are lipid abnormalities a marker of metabolic dysfunction or directly causal? | Lipid panel, particle size (if indicated), family history, guideline-based risk assessment. |
| Is endothelial function impaired? | Flow-mediated dilation or other vascular testing; specialist interpretation. |

### 4.3  Metabolic ↔ lifestyle

| Question | Evidence need |
|---|---|
| How do sleep duration/quality, physical activity, and dietary pattern relate to glycemic control? | Sleep diary, activity log, dietary assessment; behavior-change evidence from RCTs. |

---

## 5  Endocrine system ↔ other systems

### 5.1  HPA axis ↔ metabolism and immunity

| Question | Evidence need |
|---|---|
| Is cortisol rhythm dysregulated in a way that could affect sleep, appetite, or immune function? | Salivary cortisol curve or serum testing; clinical context. |
| Is "adrenal fatigue" being used as an explanation? | This is a contested framework; prefer HPA-axis dysfunction language and endocrine referral. |

### 5.2  Thyroid ↔ female hormones

| Question | Evidence need |
|---|---|
| Are thyroid and sex-hormone changes coincident (e.g., perimenopause, PCOS, subclinical hypothyroidism)? | Full thyroid and sex-hormone panels; gynecology/endocrinology interpretation. |

---

## 6  Neurobehavioral system ↔ other systems

| Question | Evidence need |
|---|---|
| Are mood, cognition, or pain symptoms linked to inflammation, mitochondrial function, or gut symptoms? | Mechanistic plausibility only; clinical evaluation for psychiatric or neurological causes first. |
| Is stress a trigger for digestive, immune, or metabolic flare? | Temporal diary; stress assessment; confounders. |

---

## 7  Circulatory system ↔ other systems

| Question | Evidence need |
|---|---|
| Is vascular inflammation or oxidative stress hypothesized? | hs-CRP, oxidized LDL (if available), endothelial function testing; guideline-based cardiovascular risk assessment. |
| Are nutrient-transport or membrane-composition issues relevant? | Red-blood-cell fatty acids or membrane studies are research tools, not routine clinical action. |

---

## 8  Lifestyle as a cross-cutting domain

| Factor | Relationship questions | Evidence need |
|---|---|---|
| Diet | Does the dietary pattern provide adequate fiber, micronutrients, and protein? Are there suspected food triggers? | Dietary assessment; elimination diets only under professional guidance. |
| Sleep | Does sleep loss worsen metabolic, immune, or mood findings? | Sleep diary or actigraphy; sleep-disorder screening. |
| Movement | Is sedentary behavior or overtraining relevant? | Activity log; exercise prescription by qualified professional only. |
| Stress / coping | Is chronic stress a mediator? | Validated stress scales, social support assessment. |
| Environment | Are toxin, mold, or chemical exposures documented? | Exposure history, environmental testing; not a basis for DIY detox. |
| Relationships | Are social determinants affecting access to food, care, or adherence? | Social history; care-coordination resources. |

---

## 9  Evidence-debt summary

The following connections are common in functional-medicine teaching but remain
**evidence debts** until independently verified:

- Gut permeability → autoimmune disease.
- HPA-axis dysregulation → metabolic syndrome.
- SNP patterns → personalized supplement dosing.
- Detoxification capacity → chronic disease.
- Oxidative stress → clinical symptom resolution after antioxidant supplementation.

These debts should be recorded on every evidence card and in the review output.
