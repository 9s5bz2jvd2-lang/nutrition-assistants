# Glossary — Key Terms from the DGA and 2025 DGAC

Adapted from DGA.gov and the 2025 DGAC Scientific Report. Definitions are operational summaries, not verbatim copies.

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Cup Equivalent (cup eq)** | Amount of a food product considered equal to 1 cup from the vegetable, fruit, or milk food group. May differ from a measured cup due to concentration (raisins, tomato paste), airiness (salad greens), or different form (cheese). |
| **The Departments** | USDA and HHS, which co-publish the DGA every five years, alternating leadership. |
| **DGA** | Dietary Guidelines for Americans. Legally mandated to reflect the "preponderance of scientific and medical knowledge." Written for professional audiences (policymakers, healthcare providers, nutrition educators); adapted for consumers. |
| **DGAC** | Dietary Guidelines Advisory Committee. Nationally recognized scientific experts who volunteer to review nutrition science collectively (not individual studies or personal testimonies). Uses systematic reviews, data analysis, and food pattern modeling. Publishes a Scientific Report for USDA/HHS. |
| **Dietary Pattern** | The combination of foods and beverages constituting an individual's complete dietary intake over time. May describe customary eating or recommended intake. |
| **Nutrient-Dense** | Foods and beverages providing vitamins, minerals, and health-promoting components with no or little added sugars, saturated fat, and sodium. Examples: vegetables, fruits, whole grains, seafood, eggs, beans/peas/lentils, unsalted nuts/seeds, fat-free/low-fat dairy, lean meats/poultry — when prepared without added sugars, saturated fat, or sodium. |
| **Ounce Equivalent (oz eq)** | Amount of a food product considered equal to 1 ounce from the Grains or Protein Foods group. May be less than a measured ounce (concentrated: nuts, peanut butter, dried meats, flour) or more (high-water: tofu, cooked beans, cooked rice/pasta). |
| **Overarching Guidelines** | User-friendly summary of key DGA recommendations, as seen in the Executive Summary. The 2025 DGAC's "Overarching Advice to the Departments" integrates findings across data analysis, systematic reviews, and food pattern modeling. |
| **Processed Meat** | Meat, poultry, or seafood preserved by smoking, curing, salting, or chemical preservatives. Examples: bacon, sausage, hot dogs, sandwich meat, packaged ham, pepperoni, salami. |
| **Red Meat** | All mammalian muscle meat from adult and juvenile animals, including cows, pigs, sheep, and goats. |
| **Scientific Report** | The DGAC's final results and recommendations submitted to USDA and HHS. The 2025 DGAC Scientific Report was submitted December 2024; public comment period was December 11, 2024 – February 10, 2025. |
| **Eat Healthy Your Way** | The 2025 DGAC's recommended name for the consolidated, flexible dietary pattern replacing the three separate patterns from the 2020–2025 DGA. The DGAC recommends the Departments conduct consumer research to finalize the name. |
| **Sustainable Healthy Diets** (FAO/WHO) | Dietary patterns that promote all dimensions of health and wellbeing; have low environmental pressure and impact; are accessible, affordable, safe, equitable; and are culturally acceptable. |
