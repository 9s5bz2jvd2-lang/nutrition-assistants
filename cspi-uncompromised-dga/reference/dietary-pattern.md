# The "Eat Healthy Your Way" Dietary Pattern

## Overview

The 2025 DGAC recommends consolidating three separate dietary patterns from the 2020–2025 DGA (Healthy U.S.–Style, Healthy Mediterranean–Style, Healthy Vegetarian) into a **single, flexible dietary pattern** called "Eat Healthy Your Way."

**Rationale:** Strong evidence shows healthy dietary patterns share core features — emphasizing fruits, vegetables, whole grains, legumes, nuts, and seeds, and limiting added sugars, saturated fat, and sodium — while allowing cultural, personal, and economic adaptation.

## 2,000-Calorie Pattern (Ages 2+)

| Food Group / Subgroup | Amount | Unit |
|---|---|---|
| **Vegetables** | 2¼ | cup eq/day |
| → Dark-Green Vegetables | 1½ | cup eq/week |
| → Red and Orange Vegetables | 5½ | cup eq/week |
| → Starchy Vegetables | 4 | cup eq/week |
| → Other Vegetables | 4 | cup eq/week |
| **Fruits** | 2 | cup eq/day |
| **Grains** | 6 | ounce eq/day |
| → Whole Grains | ≥3 | ounce eq/day |
| → Refined Grains | <3 | ounce eq/day |
| **Dairy and Fortified Soy Alternatives** | 3 | cup eq/day |
| **Protein Foods** | 7 | ounce eq/day |
| → Beans, Peas, and Lentils | 10 | ounce eq/week |
| → Nuts, Seeds, and Soy Products | 4½ | ounce eq/week |
| → Seafood | 8 | ounce eq/week |
| → Meats, Poultry, and Eggs | 26 | ounce eq/week |
| **Oils** | 27 | grams/day |

### Key direction shifts (2025 DGAC updates)

| Shift from | Shift to |
|---|---|
| Refined grains | Whole grains (≥3 oz eq/day; <3 oz eq refined) |
| Red and processed meat | Beans, peas, lentils, nuts, seeds, soy products |
| General "protein" | Prioritize plant-based proteins within protein group |
| Three separate patterns | One flexible "Eat Healthy Your Way" pattern |

### Beans, Peas, and Lentils conversion rule
- When counted within **Vegetables**: measured in cup equivalents
- To convert to **Protein Foods** ounce equivalents: multiply cup eq by 4
  - (1 cup eq = 4 ounce eq)

### Children 12–23 months
- Pattern reflects the 2020 HUSS including a lacto-ovo variation
- The DGAC did not recommend modifications to food group/subgroup quantities for this age group (only a name change)
- Children's nutrient needs are high relative to low energy needs — careful attention to food group quantities is essential

## Before age 2 (dairy-specific)
- Plain cow milk (whole milk) or fortified unsweetened soy beverage can be offered beginning around 12 months of age
- Fat-free or low-fat milk recommended starting at age 2

## Verification checklist

When answering a question about the dietary pattern:
- [ ] State the calorie level (2,000 is default; other levels in full DGAC Table E.1.2)
- [ ] Specify daily vs. weekly amounts
- [ ] Note the beans/peas/lentils conversion rule if relevant
- [ ] Note the direction shifts (whole grains ↑, red meat ↓, plant protein ↑)
- [ ] Clarify age group (2+ default; <2 and 12–23 months have specific guidance)
