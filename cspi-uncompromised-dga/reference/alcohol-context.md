# Alcohol Guidance Context

## The political timeline

| Year | Event | Key detail |
|---|---|---|
| 2015 | 2015–2020 DGA published | "Up to 1 drink/day for women, up to 2 drinks/day for men" |
| 2020 | 2020 DGAC reviews alcohol evidence | Concludes: "Up to 1 drink/day for **both** women and men"; "drinking less is better"; "do not begin or continue drinking for health" |
| 2020 | Departments finalize 2020–2025 DGA | **Rejects** the 2020 DGAC's alcohol recommendations; largely maintains 2015 guidance (2 drinks/day for men) |
| 2021 | Departments set 2025 DGAC questions | **Exclude** alcohol from DGAC review scope; delegate to two external committees |
| 2024 Dec | NASEM report published | Found moderate consumption associated with lower all-cause mortality but higher breast cancer risk; **no consumption recommendations** |
| 2025 Jan | ICCPUD draft report published | Found risk of alcohol-attributable death increases linearly with consumption; alcohol associated with increased mortality from 7 cancer types |
| 2025 | ICCPUD final report | **Never finalized** due to substantial industry lobbying and Congressional interference |
| 2025–2030 DGA | Final official version | Alcohol guidance does not fully reflect the available evidence |

## The uncompromised position

The Uncompromised DGA includes the **2020 DGAC's** alcohol recommendations because:
1. They are the most recent uncompromised, evidence-based recommendations
2. The 2025 DGAC deferred to two committees, one of which never finalized its report
3. The NASEM report made no consumption recommendations
4. The ICCPUD draft findings (linear risk increase, 7 cancer types) were consistent with tightening guidance

## The three key alcohol recommendations

1. **Do not begin to drink alcohol or purposefully continue to drink because you think it will make you healthier.**
2. **At all levels of consumption, drinking less is generally better for health than drinking more.**
3. **For those who drink alcohol, recommended limits are up to 1 drink per day for both women and men.**

## Verification note

> The alcohol guidance in this skill reflects the 2020 DGAC's evidence-based recommendations, which were not adopted by the Departments. The official published DGA may differ. When discussing alcohol guidance, always clarify which version you are referencing.

## Red flags

- Do not present the 1 drink/day limit as the official 2025–2030 DGA position (it was not adopted)
- Do not present alcohol as health-promoting (the 2020 DGAC explicitly advises against starting or continuing for health reasons)
- Mark any specific cancer risk claims with the source: "ICCPUD draft report (never finalized)"
- The ICCPUD findings about 7 cancer types and linear risk increase are from an unreviewed draft — label them accordingly
