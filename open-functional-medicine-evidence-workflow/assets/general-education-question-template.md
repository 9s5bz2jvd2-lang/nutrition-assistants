> general questions about functional-medicine concepts. It must not be used for
> personal diagnosis, treatment, supplement dosing, or lab interpretation.

## Topic

_e.g., gut barrier, insulin resistance, thyroid conversion, oxidative stress_

## Safe general question

> "What is the current evidence status of [topic] as a factor in [condition]?"

## Why this is safe

- It asks about evidence, not personal medical advice.
- It does not request a diagnosis, prescription, or supplement dose.
- It does not include any real or de-identified patient information.

## Evidence-governance checklist

- [ ] The question can be answered using public, citable sources.
- [ ] Any textbook or framework claim is tagged `textbook-claim` and not presented as verified evidence.
- [ ] Any high-risk topic is redirected to a licensed healthcare provider.
- [ ] The answer includes uncertainty and evidence debt.

## Example answer scaffold

1. **Definition** — brief, original wording.
2. **Evidence status** — what is known, what is debated, what is missing.
3. **Safety note** — why this concept is not a basis for self-treatment.
4. **Questions for a clinician** — how a learner might discuss this with a provider.
