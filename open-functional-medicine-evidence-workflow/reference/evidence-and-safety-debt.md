# Evidence and Safety Debt

> **Purpose.** Catalog disputed, high-risk, or forbidden-to-action topics
> encountered in the old functional-medicine study corpus. The old corpus may
> inform query planning, but its formulas, cases and dose details must never be
> transferred as actionable evidence. A current plan must be independently
> re-derived under the evidence and class-gate contracts. This document contains no real or de-identified patient
> information.

---

## 1  How to use this document

When any workflow mode touches a topic below, the Skill must:

1. Explain why the topic is contested or high-risk.
2. A disputed topic must not automatically become a diagnosis merely because it appears in the old corpus.
3. In individual diagnostic-support mode, keep it visibly disputed, test standard alternatives first, and record support, contradiction, discriminators, and evidence debt.
4. Refuse autonomous treatment/prescribing, medication actions, unreviewed dosing and direct transfer of old-corpus regimens. Independently derived class-gated drafts remain subject to `formula-class-gates.md`.
5. Require the licensed reviewer to approve, revise, or reject any related differential entry.

## 2  High-risk / forbidden-to-action topics

### 2.1  Supplements, dosages, and formulas

| Topic | Why it is high-risk | What the Skill may do |
|---|---|---|
| Specific nutrient or supplement dosing regimens from the old corpus | Toxicity, contraindication and interaction risks vary by individual; the old material is not a current source. | Do not reproduce or copy the regimen. If the user explicitly requests a current personalized plan, re-derive indication, form/range, interactions and monitoring from verified current sources under the supplement gate; otherwise give food/reference-intake education or an incomplete scaffold. |
| Multi-nutrient "prescription" formulas | Can imply individualized treatment without clinical assessment and compound interaction/duplication risk. | Never transfer an old formula. A new candidate may be drafted only from independently verified sources after core intake and reviewer gates; it is not executable before qualified review. |
| Stage-based supplement protocols | Prescriptive sequencing may lack evidence of safety/efficacy in the individual. | Treat the old sequence as evidence debt. Do not reproduce it; any new sequence requires independently verified evidence, explicit rationale and the class-specific reviewer gate. |
| SNP-based supplement adjustments (e.g., MTHFR → methylfolate dose) | Genetic association does not establish clinical need or dose. | Refuse SNP-deterministic dosing; explain that genetic interpretation requires specialist oversight. |

### 2.2  Special populations

| Population | Why extra caution is required |
|---|---|
| Pregnancy / lactation | Many supplements and interventions are contraindicated; needs obstetric oversight. |
| Infants, children, adolescents | Growth, dosing, and developmental considerations require pediatric evaluation. |
| Older adults | Polypharmacy, renal/hepatic changes, fall risk, and frailty alter safety profiles. |
| Severe liver or kidney disease | Nutrient metabolism and excretion are impaired; specialist care required. |
| People on prescription medications | Supplement-drug interactions are common and potentially serious. |
| Known allergies or eating disorders | Restrictive diets or supplement trials can be harmful. |

### 2.3  Contested frameworks

| Framework | Status | Skill response |
|---|---|---|
| "Leaky gut" / intestinal permeability as a root cause of multiple diseases | `contested-hypothesis` — mechanisms are plausible, but diagnostic standards and causal strength are debated. | Do not present it as an established diagnosis. If relevant, record it only as a disputed question and prioritize guideline-recognized gastrointestinal differentials; never prescribe a repair protocol. |
| "Adrenal fatigue" | `contested-hypothesis` — not a recognized endocrine diagnosis; HPA-axis dysfunction is a more cautious research framing. | Do not rank it as a recognized diagnosis. Evaluate established endocrine, sleep, psychiatric, medication, nutritional, and systemic alternatives through licensed review. |
| "Detox" / "toxins" as a disease explanation | `high-risk-not-actionable` — vague terminology; chelation, sauna detox, and home detox kits can be harmful. | Refuse detox protocols; ask about documented exposures and refer to occupational/environmental medicine. |
| Food IgG testing for chronic food sensitivity | `contested-hypothesis` — clinical utility is not established in mainstream allergy practice. | Explain controversy; suggest evidence-based allergy/elimination-diet evaluation by a professional. |
| SNP → disease → treatment chains | `high-risk-not-actionable` — most SNP associations are weak and not clinically actionable. | Refuse personalized treatment based on SNPs; encourage specialist genetic counseling if relevant. |

### 2.4  Mechanism-to-efficacy claims

| Claim pattern | Why it is debt |
|---|---|
| "X improves mitochondrial function, therefore it treats fatigue." | Mechanistic plausibility does not prove clinical benefit in humans. |
| "Y reduces oxidative stress, therefore it prevents cardiovascular disease." | Antioxidant trials have often failed to show the predicted clinical benefit. |
| "Z modulates inflammation, therefore it improves depression." | Psychoneuroimmunology is an active research area; clinical applications are experimental. |

These must be labeled `mechanism ≠ efficacy` and recorded as evidence debt.

## 3  Old cases and clinical examples

The old corpus contains detailed patient cases with histories, supplement
protocols, and follow-up outcomes. These are **learning material only** and must
not be reproduced or used as templates.

- Do not reuse patient vignettes, names (even pseudonyms), or timelines.
- Do not reproduce supplement recipes or dose schedules from cases.
- Do not cite case outcomes as evidence that an intervention works.
- New synthetic examples must be clearly labeled as fictional/hypothetical and must not copy source cases.

---

## 4  Red-flag symptoms that require immediate referral

The following are not evidence-debt items; they are **hard stops**. See
`reference/scope-and-red-lines.md` for the full red-flag list.

- Chest pain, stroke signs, severe shortness of breath, uncontrolled bleeding.
- Loss of consciousness, severe headache, anaphylaxis signs.
- Suicidal ideation, suspected poisoning/overdose.

## 5  Evidence-debt register template

Each review output should include a section like this:

| Topic | Why it is debt | What would reduce uncertainty |
|---|---|---|
| e.g., gut permeability → autoimmune disease | Causal chain not established; assay reproducibility limited | RCTs of permeability-reducing interventions in defined populations; standardized testing. |
| e.g., adrenal fatigue diagnosis | Not recognized in endocrinology guidelines | HPA-axis studies with clinically meaningful outcomes. |
